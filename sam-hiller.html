Ice In Her Veins: Firefighter Dons Olympic Hockey Referee Uniform

By David A. F. Sweet  •  February 8, 2026

Lake Forest firefighter Samantha Hiller is used to the heat of the blaze, but this winter, she’s embracing the chill of the ice. Stepping onto the world stage at the Olympic Games, Hiller has realized a lifelong dream, not as a player, but as a top-tier hockey referee.

Samantha Hiller ready for the Olympic stage.

For Hiller, the road to the Olympics was paved with thousands of hours on local skating rinks and a unwavering dedication to the sport. Balancing her demanding career as a firefighter with the rigorous training required for officiating at the highest level, she has become a symbol of excellence and versatility.

"It's about focus and fairness," Hiller says of her role on the ice. "In the firehouse, we rely on teamwork and precision. On the ice, those same principles apply. You have to maintain your cool under pressure, whether you're facing a four-alarm fire or a high-stakes breakaway."

Balancing the heat of the firehouse with the cool of the rink.
Maintaining focus during a high-speed play.

As she watches the puck drop in the Olympic arena, Hiller represents the spirit of Classic Chicago: a blend of grit, grace, and an unstoppable drive to reach new heights.
